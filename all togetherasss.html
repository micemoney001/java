HISTROY OF JAVASCRIPT IN SHORT

ES1:

Released in June 1997, ES1 was the first standardized version of JavaScript.

It introduced foundational features such as variables, functions, conditionals, and loops.

ES2 (ECMAScript 2):

- Released in June 1998, ES2 had minor updates and improvements, but no significant new features.

ES3 (ECMAScript 3):

- Released in December 1999, ES3 brought substantial improvements to JavaScript.

- It introduced features like try/catch for error handling, regular expressions, and support for working with dates and arrays.

ES4 (ECMAScript 4):

- ES4 was initially planned to be the next major release, but due to disagreements in the community, it was abandoned. No unique features were introduced in this version.

ES5 (ECMAScript 5):

- Released in December 2009, ES5 added several important features.

- It introduced strict mode for better error-checking, new array methods like forEach and map, and JSON support.

ES6 (ECMAScript 2015):

- Released in June 2015, ES6 was a significant milestone for JavaScript.

- It introduced numerous new features like arrow functions, classes, template literals (using backtick quotes), destructuring assignments, and enhanced object literals.

- ES6 also introduced modules for better code organization and introduced new iterations and generators for advanced looping and control flow. ES6 marked a shift in JavaScript's development, with subsequent yearly releases (ES2016, ES2017, etc.) bringing further enhancements and new features to the language.



DIFFERENT BETWEEN COMPARISON AND LOGICAL

COMPARISON

(1.) Comparison operators are used to compare two values and evaluate whether they are equal, not equal, greater than, less than, etc. Examples include "==" for equal to, "!=" for not equal to, ">" for greater than, "<" for less than.
(2.) They allow you to easily compare values and determine their relationships.
(3.) They allow you to validate user input by comparing it with expected values.
(4.) Comparison operators are crucial in sorting and searching algorithms.
(5.) They enable you to perform mathematical operations by comparing values.
(6.) Comparison operators are essential in creating loops and iterating over data.
(7.) Comparison operators help in making decisions and controlling the flow of a program.

LOGICAL

Logical operators, on the other hand, are used to combine multiple conditions and evaluate them as a whole. Examples include "&&" for logical AND, "||" for logical OR, and "!" for logical NOT.
(2.) They help in creating complex decision-making processes by combining multiple criteria.
(3.) Logical operators enable you to control the flow of your program based on different conditions.
(4.) They provide a concise and efficient way to express compound conditions.
(5.)Logical operators are essential in creating loops and iterating over data based on specific conditions.
(6.)They help in filtering and organizing data by applying multiple criteria.
(7.) Logical operators are widely used in conditional statements like if-else and switch-case.
